Week 12 — Capstone

Phase 4 · Python & Capstone Skill Focus: All Five, Integrated ~15 hours
Course Progress
Week 12 of 12

One project. Real data. Every tool from the course. Every skill you've drilled. By the end of this week you have a portfolio piece that looks like the work a senior analyst ships. The capstone is the top item in your portfolio and probably the first thing you mention in every interview from here forward.

Learning Goals

  • Execute a full end-to-end analysis using SQL, pandas, Looker Studio, and written interpretation
  • Integrate all five AI co-work skills in a single project
  • Work with three real public data sources (SEC EDGAR + FRED + US Treasury Fiscal Data)
  • Produce an interview-ready walkthrough of the project
  • Ship the capstone as the top entry in your portfolio

The Capstone Question

“How did public companies manage working capital through the 2022-2025 rate-hike cycle?”

This question is good for three reasons:

  1. It's specific enough to answer in one week of work.
  2. It requires three separate data sources, which forces real joining and cleaning.
  3. It directly surfaces your treasury domain expertise. Anyone can plot rates going up. Only someone with working capital experience can tell you what it meant for how companies operated.

That combination is your differentiator. The capstone proves you have it.

Why This Data Combination Matters

Bootcamp capstones typically use a single Kaggle dataset and look like every other bootcamp capstone. Your combination of SEC EDGAR company financials + FRED macro indicators + US Treasury Fiscal Data yields is, as far as the career-pivoter cohort goes, unique.

SEC EDGAR gives you working capital, cash, receivables, payables for any public company. FRED gives you the Fed Funds rate, inflation, PMI, and every other macro indicator. US Treasury Fiscal Data gives you daily yield curves and federal spending as the macro backdrop.

Combining them lets you tell a story that Stephen-the-treasurer would naturally tell: how companies adjusted cash management as rates moved. That's a treasury analyst thinking out loud, on paper, with data.

AI Co-Work Pattern — All Five Skills Integrated

The full loop, every day this week

  1. Translate: what question am I answering today? (might be a sub-question of the main capstone)
  2. Command: draft a SQL query, pandas cell, or chart with Claude
  3. Validate: check the output against domain expectation
  4. Iterate: fix whatever isn't right, push back on Claude, refine
  5. Present: write the one-sentence takeaway before moving on

You've drilled each of these separately. This week they run in the same morning. You'll feel the flow by mid-week.

The Week in Five Stages

Stage 1 (Mon-Tue) — Data acquisition and setup

Pull the three data sources, get them into a workable format, upload to your Week 12 Claude Project.

Stage 2 (Tue-Wed) — SQL layer

Use SQL (in DB Fiddle or pandas-sql) to unify the three sources into analytical tables.

Stage 3 (Wed-Thu) — Pandas analysis

Do the actual analysis in a Colab notebook: compute working capital metrics per company per quarter, correlate to rate levels, find the patterns.

Stage 4 (Thu-Fri) — Dashboard and visuals

Build a Looker Studio dashboard that tells the story. Also export matplotlib charts for the written memo.

Stage 5 (Fri-Sat) — Written memo + interview walkthrough

Write the 2,000-word case study. Record (or write out) the 5-minute interview walkthrough.

Stage 1 — Data Acquisition

Source 1: SEC EDGAR Financial Statement Data Sets

Go to SEC EDGAR Financial Statement Data Sets. Download quarterly ZIPs from 2022 Q1 through 2025 Q4. Each ZIP contains tagged line items for every public company filing that quarter.

You don't need every company. Pick 8-10 companies across different industries for the capstone. Good candidates: Apple, Microsoft, Walmart, Costco, Starbucks, Target, Home Depot, Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola, Johnson & Johnson. Use their 10-K and 10-Q filings.

Source 2: FRED Macroeconomic Data

Go to FRED. Download these series as CSV (free, no API key needed):

  • Federal Funds Effective Rate (FEDFUNDS)
  • 10-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Rate (DGS10)
  • Consumer Price Index (CPIAUCSL)
  • Industrial Production Index (INDPRO)

2020-2025 daily or monthly, your call. Monthly is usually sufficient.

Source 3: US Treasury Fiscal Data

Go to US Treasury Fiscal Data. Pull:

  • Daily Treasury Par Yield Curve Rates (the full curve)
  • Monthly Treasury Statement (federal receipts and outlays)

This gives you the full yield curve context around company cash positions.

Setup Claude Project

Create “Stephen Data Course — Capstone” Project. Upload all the CSVs. Update project instructions:

Capstone project: working capital management during the 2022-2025
rate-hike cycle. Data sources: SEC EDGAR 10-K/10-Q for 8-10 public
companies, FRED macro data (Fed Funds, 10Y Treasury, CPI, IP), US
Treasury Fiscal Data (yield curve, federal flows).

Target role context: Stephen has 15 years treasury experience. The
capstone is both a learning artifact and a portfolio anchor. The
analysis should read like an analyst briefing a treasurer, not a
bootcamp graduation project.

Stage 2 — SQL Unification

The three sources are messy. Different formats. Different time granularities. Different join keys. SQL is the right tool for getting them into unified analytical tables.

Target schema (design it first)

  1. company_quarterly: company_id, ticker, quarter_end, revenue, total_assets, current_assets, current_liabilities, cash, ar, ap, inventory
  2. macro_monthly: month_end, fed_funds_rate, treasury_10y, cpi_yoy, ip_index
  3. yield_curve_daily: date, maturity, rate

Build with Claude

I have three raw CSVs uploaded:
- sec_edgar_2022_2025.csv (raw quarterly data for 10 companies)
- fred_macro.csv (4 series, monthly)
- treasury_yields.csv (daily yield curve)

I need to transform them into these three analytical tables: [paste
target schema]. Walk me through:

1. The cleaning each raw file needs (date parsing, null handling,
   unit normalization)
2. The SQL to build each analytical table
3. The quality checks to run before trusting each table (row counts,
   date ranges, obvious outliers)

Build the SQL one table at a time. After each, I'll run it in DB
Fiddle (or pandas.read_sql) and validate the row count matches what
I'd expect.

Stage 3 — Pandas Analysis

Now the real analysis. In Colab. With pandas.

Metrics to compute

  • Working capital per company per quarter: current_assets - current_liabilities
  • Cash conversion cycle (DSO + DIO - DPO) per company per quarter
  • Cash / current_liabilities ratio (defensive liquidity measure)
  • Merge in macro rates as of each quarter-end
  • Correlation of working capital change vs rate change, per company

Build with Claude

I have three CSVs loaded into pandas from the SQL stage. Build an
analysis notebook that:

1. Computes quarterly working capital per company
2. Computes cash conversion cycle per company per quarter
3. Merges each company-quarter with the macro rates in that quarter
4. Runs correlation analysis: for each company, how strongly does
   working capital change track with rate change?
5. Groups companies by industry (I'll provide the mapping) and
   compares patterns

Build each section as its own notebook cell. Validate row counts
and sensibility checks after each section. Use matplotlib for 3
inline charts:
- Working capital by industry over time
- Fed Funds rate vs average cash/CL ratio across companies
- Cash conversion cycle distribution by quarter (box plot)

Stage 4 — Dashboard and Visuals

Build a Looker Studio dashboard that tells the capstone's story at three levels.

Three pages

  1. The Story: one big chart showing the rate cycle alongside average working capital trend. One sentence conclusion at the top.
  2. Per-Company Detail: filter dropdown for company. Shows that company's working capital, CCC, and cash position over time with rate overlay.
  3. Industry Comparison: small multiples showing industry-level working capital trends side by side.

Publish and link

Publish as “anyone with the link can view.” Test in incognito. Grab the link for your README.

Stage 5 — Written Memo + Interview Walkthrough

The written memo (~2,000 words)

Structure:

  1. Executive summary (200 words) — the one-paragraph version. Top-of-page.
  2. The question and why it matters (300 words) — frame the problem from a treasurer's perspective
  3. Data and methodology (400 words) — what sources, what transformations, what caveats
  4. Findings (700 words) — the three most important things the analysis showed, with charts
  5. What surprised me (200 words) — specific, honest, not manufactured
  6. What I'd do with another week (200 words) — shows self-awareness

The interview walkthrough (5 minutes)

Practice this out loud. Record yourself if you can. The structure:

  1. “I built a capstone project on working capital management during the 2022-2025 rate cycle.” (15 seconds, the hook)
  2. “I combined three data sources: [brief]” (30 seconds, the data)
  3. “The methodology was: [brief]” (60 seconds, SQL + pandas + dashboard)
  4. “The key finding was: [specific, with a number]” (90 seconds, the insight)
  5. “What surprised me was: [specific]” (45 seconds, shows depth)
  6. “If I had more time I'd: [specific]” (30 seconds, self-awareness)
  7. “Here's the link if you want to walk through the dashboard with me.” (10 seconds, the ask)

Practice this with Claude as your mock interviewer. Get sharp before the real interview.
