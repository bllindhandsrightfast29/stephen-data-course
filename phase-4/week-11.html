Week 11 — Analytics-Grade Pandas

Phase 4 · PythonSkill Focus: Iterate with AI~14–17 hours
Course Progress
Week 11 of 12

Time series in pandas. Reshaping. Multi-source merging. Plotting from pandas directly. Build the Treasury Liquidity Monitor using FRED data. You are likely interviewing by now — this notebook is walk-through-able in 5 minutes.

Learning Goals

  • resample, rolling, shift for time series
  • pivot, melt, stack, unstack for reshaping
  • Drill Skill #4 again — iterate multi-step analyses with Claude without rewriting from scratch
  • Ship: Treasury Liquidity Monitor notebook + 500-word interpretation writeup

Content Releases After Week 10

Time-series pattern, FRED API walkthrough, and interpretation-writeup template unlock after Week 10 commit.

AI Co-Work Pattern (Preview)

Skill #4 in pandas context. When Claude's first answer is 70% right, do not rewrite it yourself — describe what's wrong and let Claude patch. Never accept 50% right. Keep iterating until the output holds up to validation.

Resources (Preview)

  • Kaggle Pandas (finish if not complete)
  • Wes McKinney “Python for Data Analysis” (free online chapters)
  • YouTube: Corey Schafer pandas playlist (free, excellent)
  • ML-for-Trading (stale 2024-08, inspiration only)

Mini-Project (Preview) — Treasury Liquidity Monitor

Five FRED series over 5 years: 3-month yield, 10-year yield, Effective Fed Funds Rate, CPI, unemployment. Align on monthly index. Compute yield-curve spread, real interest rate, rolling 3-month and 12-month correlations. Plot with matplotlib. Output a printable “current state” summary table. The 500-word interpretation is the deliverable that shows domain reasoning, not just plotting.
