Job Search Kit

Runs in parallel with Weeks 9–12. Target: $120–160k mid-market analyst roles where treasury and cash-management experience is directly relevant.

LinkedIn Rewrite Checklist

Headline (under 220 characters)

Structure: “[Current-role target] | [Prior-role credibility] | [Tool stack] | [Differentiator]”. Avoid “aspiring,” “seeking,” “in training” — those signal weakness.

[STEPHEN WRITES — draft a headline in your own words and iterate it with Claude using the template structure above]

About section — three paragraphs

Draft three paragraphs in your own voice, then iterate with Claude using the Skill #5 prompts. Rough shape:

  • Paragraph 1 — who you are. Years of experience, the specific finance/treasury work you did, one concrete detail that shows depth.
  • Paragraph 2 — why analytics. What changed, why now, what the 12-week course covered.
  • Paragraph 3 — what you bring. The tool stack, how you work with AI day-to-day, one specific example from your portfolio.
[STEPHEN WRITES — three paragraphs in your own voice. Paste into Claude for critique on clarity and specificity. Do not let Claude rewrite the whole thing. Keep your words.]

Featured section

Pin 3 portfolio entries with 1-paragraph write-ups each. Rotate as stronger ones ship.

Resume Format

  • 1 page for 0–10 years experience, 2 pages max
  • Skills: SQL, Python (pandas), Tableau, Excel/Sheets, AI-assisted workflows (Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot), Google Colab, plus your finance domain
  • Experience: rewrite treasury bullets to emphasize analysis, not operations. “Reconciled X” becomes “Designed reconciliation workflow that caught $X variance across Y entities.”
  • Projects: 3–5 from your portfolio with 1-line descriptions

Application Rhythm (Weeks 9–12)

  • 10 applications/week minimum, 20 max
  • Each one tailored — change summary, adjust 3–5 bullets, match keywords
  • Track in a spreadsheet: company, role, date applied, source, status, follow-up date

Interview Prep

Mock interviews with Claude

I am interviewing for a data analyst role. Give me 3 technical SQL
questions at [junior / mid] level. After I answer each, critique
my answer and show me the ideal solution.

Rotate through: SQL, pandas, viz critique, case-study walkthroughs.

Mock interviews with Jon

Every 2 weeks during Weeks 10–12. Jon plays hiring manager. Real-time feedback on voice, pacing, confidence, BS detection.

Case studies to master

Three from your portfolio. Know the data, approach, decisions, limitations, and what you would do differently. Practice the walkthrough until it is 5 minutes tight.

Target Companies (Mid-Market Analyst Roles, 2026)

Denver, Raleigh, Austin, Nashville, Charlotte, Jacksonville — mid-market financials, regional banks, insurance, REITs, logistics, any industry where treasury and cash-management is part of the job. Finance-adjacent analyst roles are the warmer market for this profile.

Avoid initially

  • FAANG — traditional-path hiring pipeline, harder entry point
  • Pure SaaS startups — they tend to want product analysts, different skill tree

Target

  • Mid-market CFO orgs hiring their first dedicated analyst
  • Corp finance teams adding an analyst to an existing group
  • Treasury-as-a-service firms
  • Consulting firms with a finance analytics practice
