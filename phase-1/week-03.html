Week 3 — Financial Analysis Formalized

Phase 1 · FoundationSkill Focus: Validate AI Output~15 hours
Course Progress
Week 3 of 12

Translate your treasury instincts into standard financial-analysis vocabulary: ratio analysis, DuPont, common-size statements, variance, trend decomposition. Position your resume to name-drop these frameworks.

Learning Goals

  • Fluency with 8+ core financial ratios (liquidity, leverage, efficiency, profitability)
  • Drill Skill #3: validate Claude's output against your domain knowledge
  • Ship a three-company comparative analysis using real 10-Ks

Content Releases After Week 2

Full drill content, mini-project details, and portfolio template will unlock once Week 2's workbook is committed.

AI Co-Work Pattern (Preview)

Skill #3: Claude will tell you things about financial statements. Some will be right. Some will be oversimplified or wrong. Your job is to catch the bad ones. Example:

Claude, explain the DuPont identity and give me a worked
example on Apple's FY2024 financials.

Claude returns an explanation with numbers. You open Apple's actual 10-K on SEC EDGAR and check the math. Report back: “You said ROE = 170%. The 10-K gives 157%. Show me the discrepancy.” Iterate.

Resources (Preview)

  • CFI free tier
  • SEC EDGAR
  • Aswath Damodaran free YouTube lectures (NYU Stern)
  • Financial Times free-tier articles for current-events ratio analysis

Mini-Project (Preview)

Pick three public companies (one strong, one mediocre, one struggling). Pull three years of 10-Ks. For each: eight core ratios, DuPont decomposition, common-size income statement, variance analysis, plus a 500-word “treasury reviewer's note” for the CFO. This is the first portfolio entry that looks like hire-able analyst work.
