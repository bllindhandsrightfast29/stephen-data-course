Week 1 — Claude as Pair Partner

Phase 1 · Foundation Skill Focus: Translate Problem → Data Question ~8 hours
Course Progress
Week 1 of 12

This week you set up Claude as your workspace and run your first real analyst drill inside it. No notebook app. No external tools. No software installs. You get comfortable commanding Claude, because every week after this one uses it.

Learning Goals

  • Set up a Claude account and your first Project with course-specific instructions
  • Drill Skill #1: translate a treasury business problem into a precise, SQL-able data question — entirely inside Claude
  • Ship your first public GitHub portfolio entry, drafted in Claude and exported

Setup — Do This Monday

Three accounts, ten minutes. You stay on the free tier this week. You will upgrade to Claude Pro in Week 2 when you start uploading real spreadsheets for analysis.

1. Claude account (free tier is fine)

Go to claude.ai. Sign up with email or Google. Free tier gives you enough daily messages for this week's work. You will hit limits in Week 2 and upgrade then, not now.

2. Create a Project for this course

In the Claude sidebar, click Projects then Create Project. Name it Stephen Data Course — Week 1. In the instructions box, paste this:

I'm Stephen Branch, working through Week 1 of a data analytics course.
My background is treasury and accounting. Use treasury and finance
examples when you explain things. I want to understand what every
line of code or formula does, don't just write it for me. When I ask
a question, explain the thinking first and then give the answer.
When I get something wrong, tell me what I'm missing. Don't flatter me.

This week's focus: translating treasury business problems into
precise, answerable data questions.

Click save. Every conversation you start inside this Project inherits these instructions.

3. GitHub account (free)

Go to github.com, sign up. Create a new public repo called stephen-data-portfolio. Saturday you push your first entry to it.

AI Co-Work Pattern — Skill #1

The habit this course is built around

Every treasury problem is secretly a data question. Your boss says “AR is climbing, why?” That's a business problem. The data question might be: “For invoices aged 60+ days in Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025, by customer segment, what is the distribution of days outstanding and has the mix shifted?” You translate. Claude does not know your business. You do.

The Drill — All Inside Claude

Open a new conversation inside your Week 1 Project. You do this drill five times this week, once per treasury question. Everything happens in chat.

Step 1 — Write your first attempt, in chat

Type into the Claude chat box:

Business problem: [1-2 sentences in plain English, from your
treasury experience]

My first attempt at a data question:
[one sentence, as precise as you can make it]

Do not critique yet. Just acknowledge you have it.

Send. Claude will acknowledge. You have now committed to a version. That's on the record.

Step 2 — Ask Claude to critique

Next message:

Now critique the data question on three dimensions:
1. Is it precise enough that someone could write SQL against it?
2. What ambiguity remains (dates, grain, filters, definitions)?
3. What are 2-3 follow-up questions that would make the analysis
   more useful?

Do not rewrite my question for me. Point at the gaps.
I will rewrite.

Read the critique. Take notes in your head, not on paper. The chat IS your notes.

Step 3 — Rewrite, still in chat

Type your v2 question into the same chat. Don't paste Claude's version. Rewrite in your own words based on the critique.

Step 4 — Stress test

Here is my rewritten data question: [yours v2]

Act as a data engineer who just received this as a ticket.
What clarifying questions would you ask before writing SQL?

Engineer-Claude has zero clarifying questions: your question is tight. Three or more: rewrite again. Loop until it's tight.

Trap to avoid

“Claude, what data questions should I write for treasury?” — that's lookup, not co-work. You would get generic answers. Your lived experience generates better questions than Claude's training data can. You drive. Claude reviews.

Mini-Project — Five Treasury Questions

The deliverable: a document titled “5 data questions I could have answered in my last treasury job.”

How to build it inside Claude

After you've run the drill five times (five separate questions), start a new conversation in the same Project and ask Claude:

I have run the four-step drill five times. Each time I landed on
a solid v2 data question. Help me compile a 1,200-word document
with five numbered sections. For each question include:

- Business problem (2 sentences, plain English)
- Data question (precise, SQL-able even if I cannot write SQL yet)
- Data required (tables, fields, grain)
- Decision informed (what would have changed with the answer?)

Format as markdown. Start with a 3-sentence intro about who I am
and why I am doing this course. End with a 1-paragraph reflection
on what I learned about my own domain by writing this.

Here are my five questions with drill notes: [paste your drill
history from the previous conversations here]

Claude returns the draft as an artifact. You read it. You rewrite the intro and the reflection in your own voice (this is the one piece that must sound like Stephen, not a model). Export the artifact as a .md file.

Commit Saturday

Push to your public GitHub repo: stephen-data-portfolio/week_01_five_treasury_questions.md. First commit. It will look spare by design. Later weeks stack on top of it.

How This Builds Forward

Every subsequent week you translate new problems into data questions. This week you drilled on home turf (treasury) so the mechanics are clear. Weeks 4+ apply the same skill to unfamiliar domains (retail sales, public debt, SaaS cohort data). If you can translate in Week 1, you can translate anywhere by Week 12.
