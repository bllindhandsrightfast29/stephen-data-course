Week 1 — Claude as Pair Partner

Phase 1 · Foundation Skill Focus: Translate Problem → Data Question ~13 hours
Course Progress
Week 1 of 12

Stop thinking of Claude as a search engine. Most people treat Claude like Google with personality — ask a question, skim the answer, move on. That is lookup. Co-work is different. This week you build the foundational habit every other week depends on.

Learning Goals

  • Build the habit of writing-before-prompting — you are the author, Claude is the senior reviewer
  • Drill Skill #1: translate a business problem into a precise, SQL-able data question
  • Ship your first public GitHub portfolio entry: “My treasury career in 5 analyses”

AI Co-Work Pattern — Skill #1

This is the course's moat — read carefully

Every treasury problem is secretly a data question wearing a suit. Your boss says “AR is climbing, why?” That is a business problem. The data question might be: “For invoices aged 60+ days in Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025, by customer segment, what is the distribution of days-outstanding and has the mix shifted?” You translate. Claude does not know your business. You do.

The Four-Step Drill

For each of your five treasury questions this week, run this exact sequence:

Step 1 — Write it yourself, no AI

In your notebook, write the business problem (1-2 sentences) and your first-cut data question (1 sentence, as precise as you can make it). Do not open Claude yet. This is the muscle.

Step 2 — Paste to Claude, ask for critique

Copy this prompt template (you will reuse it all course):

I am trying to translate a treasury business problem into a precise
data question. Here is my attempt.

Business problem: [yours]
Data question: [yours]

Critique the data question on three dimensions:
1. Is it precise enough that someone could write SQL against it?
2. What ambiguity remains (dates, grain, filters, definitions)?
3. What are 2-3 follow-up data questions that would make this
   analysis more useful?

Do not rewrite my question for me. Point at the gaps.
I will rewrite.

Step 3 — Rewrite it yourself, alone again

Based on Claude's critique, rewrite your data question. Do not paste Claude's version. Paste your rewrite back for the final check.

Step 4 — Validate with engineer-stress-test

Here is my rewritten data question: [yours v2]

Act as a data engineer who just received this as a ticket.
What clarifying questions would you ask before writing SQL?

If engineer-Claude has zero clarifying questions, your question is tight. Three or more, your question needs work.

Trap to avoid

“Claude, what data questions should I write for treasury?” — that is lookup, not co-work. You would get generic answers. Your real lived experience generates better questions than Claude's training data can.

Mini-Project — Five Treasury Questions

Write a ~1,500-word document titled “5 data questions I could have answered in my last treasury job.”

For each of the five questions, include:

  • Business problem — 2 sentences, plain English
  • Data question — precise, SQL-able even if you can't write SQL yet
  • Data required — tables, fields, grain (daily? per-invoice?)
  • Decision informed — what would have changed if you had the answer?

This is a stealth exercise. You are drilling Skill #1 on territory you know cold. By end of week you can translate any finance problem into a data question — rarer than SQL syntax, and worth more on a resume.

Portfolio Entry Template

Commit Saturday: portfolio/week_01_five_treasury_questions.md

File should contain:

  1. A short intro (3–4 sentences) — who you are, why this pivot, what this document demonstrates
  2. Five numbered sections, one per question, with the four fields above
  3. A closing paragraph: what you learned about your own domain while writing this

Push to your public GitHub repo named stephen-data-portfolio. This is your first public commit. It will look spare this week. That is fine. Week 12's entry will make this one look like a sapling next to an oak.

How This Builds Forward

Every subsequent week you will translate new problems into data questions. This week you drill on home turf (treasury) so the translation mechanics are clear. Weeks 4+ apply it to unfamiliar domains (retail, music sales, public debt) and the skill has to hold up there too. If you can translate in Week 1, you can translate anywhere by Week 12.
